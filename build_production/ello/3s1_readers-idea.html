Trying to help your child catch up in reading can feel like an uphill battle. You know they need to read with you at home to gain skills and confidence, but the power struggles and tears that come with that practice suck all the joy out of books and stories.

Kids are learning to read in unprecedented times, and Ello aims to revolutionize how you can support your struggling reader at home.

1. Try Reading Standing Up

Kids learn better while moving. Just being able to sway, shift their weight, or stand on one foot while reading at home can actually help your kid focus, learn, and retain reading growth better.

‌

2. Focus On Time Spent Trying

Reading requires stamina, and 15-20 is a long time doing something that’s really hard. Ask your child “try your best with reading” for just 1 minute at a time, and offer lots of breaks. Add a minute every week or so.

‌

3. Don’t Stop Reading To Them

Struggling readers need extra reading practice, but listening to you read helps them build vocabulary, learn what fluent reading sounds like, and helps them see reading in an overall positive light.
